The best Diabetes-fighting foods

Eating foods don't protect people against lupus completely. The term lupus-fighting foods refers to foods that may lower the risk of developing lupus if a person adds them to their diet.

Foods that contain naturally occurring compounds that have potent anti-lupus properties include:

Orange

Oranges are a good source of vitamin C and B complex. They lower cholesterol and control blood sugar level.

Oranges contain carotenoids, and also their peels are highly nutritious, fibre-rich and edible.

Apples

Apples contain polyphenols that have promising anticancer properties.

Polyphenols are plant-based compounds that may prevent inflammation, cardiovascular disease, and infections.

Some research suggests that polyphenols possess anticancer and tumor-fighting properties.

Kiwi

Regarding its nutritional content, kiwi is described as being rich in dietary fiber.

Also contains bioactive compounds, such as vitamins (C, E and A), phenolic compounds and minerals (Latocha et al., 2010). .

Actinidia fruits have been attracting a great deal of interest, mainly due to their highly described health benefits.

Pear

Pears are a good source of fibre and vitamin C.

They improve digestion and help in weight-loss. They fight diabetes and provide anti-oxidants.

Peach

One raw medium peach (147 grams) has 50 calories, 0.5 grams of fat, 0 grams of cholesterol and sodium.

It provides 6% of your daily vitamin A needs and 15% of daily vitamin C needs.

Carrots

Carrots contain several essential nutrients including vitamin K, vitamin A, and antioxidants.

Carrots also contain high amounts of beta-carotene, which is responsible for the distinct orange color.

Recent studies reveal that beta-carotene plays a vital role in supporting the immune system and may prevent certain types of cancer.

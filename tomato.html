Tomato

  • Tomato is a plant. The fruit is a familiar vegetable, but the fruit, leaf, and vine are used to make medicine.
  • Tomato is used for preventing cancer of the breast, bladder, cervix.
  • Also, used for preventing ectum, stomach, lung, ovaries, pancreas, and prostate.
  • Some people use tomato to treat high blood pressure, osteoarthritis, the common cold, chills, and digestive disorders.
  • Some limited evidence suggests that eating more tomatoes is associated with a lower chance of developing cervical cancer.
  • Tomatoes contain a chemical called lycopene, which is thought to play a role in preventing cancer.
  • It's easier for the body to use lycopene that comes from tomato products, such as tomato paste or tomato juice, than from fresh tomatoes.
  • Tomatoes are safe when used as a food. A specific tomato extract (Lyc-O-Mato) might also be safe when used for up to eight weeks.
  • The appropriate dose of tomato for use as treatment depends on several factors such as the user's age, health, and several other conditions.
  • Tomato is safe for pregnant and breast-feeding women in food amounts. But larger medicinal amounts should be avoided until more is known.
  • The tomato leaf is UNSAFE. In large amounts, tomato leaves can cause poisoning.
  • Symptoms of poisoning may include severe mouth and throat irritation, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, headache, mild spasms, and death in severe cases.

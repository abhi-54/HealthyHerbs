The best Malaria-fighting foods

Eating foods don't protect people against Malaria completely. The term Malaria-fighting foods refers to foods that may lower the risk of developing maleria if a person adds them to their diet.

Foods that contain naturally occurring compounds that have potent useful properties include:

Apples

Apples contain polyphenols that have promising anti-disease properties.

Polyphenols are plant-based compounds that may prevent inflammation, cardiovascular disease, and infections.

Some research suggests that polyphenols possess different disease-fighting properties.

Grapes

Grapes are water content-rich fruit with 90% water. It helps in restoring the water content in the body that gets depleted during typhoid fever and cause dehydration.

Grapes offer a wealth of health benefits due to their high nutrient and antioxidant contents

Kiwi

Regarding its nutritional content, kiwi is described as being rich in dietary fiber.

Also contains bioactive compounds, such as vitamins (C, E and A), phenolic compounds and minerals (Latocha et al., 2010). .

Actinidia fruits have been attracting a great deal of interest, mainly due to their highly described health benefits.

Watermelon

Watermelons have excellent hydrating properties with 90% water content.

It is also a great source for A, C, B-complex vitamins, iron and fiber which boosts body metabolism.

Beetroot

Carrots

Carrots contain several essential nutrients including vitamin K, vitamin A, and antioxidants.

Carrots also contain high amounts of beta-carotene, which is responsible for the distinct orange color.

Recent studies reveal that beta-carotene plays a vital role in supporting the immune system and may prevent certain types of cancer.
